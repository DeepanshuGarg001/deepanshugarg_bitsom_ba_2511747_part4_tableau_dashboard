# Dashboard Story

**Prepared by:** Deepanshu Garg, Business Analyst
**Audience:** Retail Leadership Team
**Period:** 2024 – 2025

---

## 1. Executive Summary
Our retail business generated **$217 million** in total sales and **$33.3 million** in profit over the analysis period, achieving an average profit margin of 13.1%. The executive dashboard tells a mixed story: top-line growth is strong and consistent, but the profitability picture is complex — heavily skewed by category performance, discounting behaviour, and logistics execution. This story walks through what is performing well, what is underperforming, and what leadership needs to act on.

---

## 2. What Is Performing Well
- **Revenue Growth:** Monthly sales show an upward trend from 2024 to 2025, driven by increasing customer acquisition across all segments.
- **Technology Category:** Copiers and Phones are the strongest profit drivers, demonstrating that customers are willing to pay a premium for technology products with minimal discounting needed.
- **South Region:** Consistently the highest-performing region by total sales, indicating strong market penetration and distribution efficiency.
- **Same-Day Shipping:** When customers select Same-Day delivery, delivery days are minimized significantly, directly correlating with higher customer satisfaction scores.

---

## 3. What Is Underperforming
- **Paper Sub-Category:** Despite being a high-volume product, Paper generates the lowest profit — it is essentially a loss-leader subsidized by better-performing products.
- **Furniture Returns:** The Furniture category has a return rate of 7.7%, nearly double the Technology return rate, suggesting product quality or delivery damage issues.
- **High Discount Leakage:** The discount vs. profit scatter analysis reveals that orders with discounts above 20–30% frequently tip into negative profit territory.

---

## 4. What Risks Are Visible
- **Margin Erosion:** With an average margin of just 13.1% and a strong negative correlation between discount and margin, unchecked discounting is the single biggest risk to profitability.
- **Logistics Risk:** 15.9% of orders take 6 or more days to deliver. In a competitive retail market, slow delivery correlates with poor reviews and elevated return risk.
- **Furniture Liability:** High return rates in Furniture represent both a direct revenue loss and an indirect brand damage risk.

---

## 5. What Opportunities Are Visible
- **Home Office Segment Expansion:** This segment generates the highest profit per customer. Developing targeted Home Office product bundles and loyalty programs could unlock significant additional revenue.
- **Regional Replication:** The strategic success drivers of the South Region (product mix, distribution) should be mapped and replicated in the North and East regions, which underperform.
- **Technology Upselling:** Since Technology drives the highest margins, cross-selling and upselling accessories (e.g., cables, warranties, peripherals) within Technology could meaningfully increase the average order value.

---

## 6. Recommended Business Actions
1. Introduce a **discount governance policy** with hard caps (max 20%) and manager approval required for any order above that threshold.
2. **Investigate and fix** the Furniture return pipeline — engage with logistics partners on improved packaging, and add better product descriptions/images to the website to reduce expectation mismatches.
3. **Expand the Home Office segment** with dedicated marketing campaigns and loyalty rewards programmes.
4. **Audit the Paper category** for profitability — consider reducing SKUs or introducing minimum order quantities to offset thin margins.

---

## 7. Limitations of the Dashboard
- The dashboard does not show **external factors** such as competitor pricing, inflation, or macroeconomic conditions, which may be partially responsible for observed trends.
- Customer-level data is aggregated; the dashboard does not show the lifetime value of individual customers.
- Campaign channel performance (Organic, Social, Referral) is visible but not deeply explored in the current views. A future iteration should include a Campaign ROI view.

---

## 8. Suggested Next Analysis
- **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) Analysis** by segment and region to understand the long-term profitability of customer acquisition.
- **Discount Sensitivity Analysis** to quantify exactly at what discount percentage profit turns negative for each product category.
- **Cohort Analysis** to understand whether 2025 customers are more or less profitable than 2024 customers.
